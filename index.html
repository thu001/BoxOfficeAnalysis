1. Introduction

Each year, the movie industry produces more movies, higher-budget movies, and more genres of media compete for audience attention. Between 2000 and 2010, only 36% of movies had profitable box-office returns. Therefore, missing an opportunity to make a commercia success movie has never been more costly than ever. And every decision the production company make has to takes more than ever into count.


Both imdb.com and tmdb.com are websites that serve as online databases of world film industry. Those websites contain a large amount of public data on films over the past few decades. Especially in the past few years, this kind of rating website has become many audiences first choice to examine the quality of movies. In addition to gaining tremendous profit commercially from a commercial success movie, a great movie also entertains the audience, and may even improve an individuals perception.


By examining the historical data from thousands of movies over several decades, we are able to get statistically meaningful results that may help provide recommendations for the industry. As the saying goes, knowledge is power, and data analytics gives us the ability to turn raw data into valuable knowledge. However, is a highly-rated movie necessarily mean to be a commercial success movie? That what we are going to discuss throughout this project.
